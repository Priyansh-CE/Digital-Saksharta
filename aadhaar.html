Aadhaar

Unique Digital Identity

What is Aadhaar?

Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identification number issued by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI). It helps citizens access government services, banking services, scholarships and various digital facilities.

Benefits of Aadhaar

Government Services

Easy access to government schemes, subsidies and digital services.

Banking Services

Helps in secure KYC verification and banking facilities.

Student Services

Useful for scholarships, admissions and education services.

Aadhaar Registration Process

01

Visit an authorised Aadhaar Enrollment Centre.

02

Fill the Aadhaar enrollment form.

03

Provide biometric details (fingerprint and iris scan).

04

Collect acknowledgement slip and track Aadhaar status.

Required Documents for Aadhaar

Identity Proof

Passport, PAN Card, Driving Licence, Voter ID or other valid identity documents.

Address Proof

Electricity Bill, Bank Statement, Passport or Government documents.

Date of Birth Proof

Birth Certificate, School Certificate or other valid documents.

How to Update Aadhaar?

Visit Centre

Visit the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra for biometric or demographic updates.

Submit Documents

Provide required documents for verification.

Biometric Verification

Fingerprint and biometric verification is completed at the centre.

Download e-Aadhaar

You can download your Aadhaar digitally using Aadhaar number, enrollment ID or Virtual ID.


Step 1

Visit the official UIDAI website.

Step 2

Enter Aadhaar details and OTP verification.

Step 3

Download your password protected e-Aadhaar PDF.

Aadhaar Safety Tips

Protect Information

Never share Aadhaar OTP, PIN or personal details with strangers.

Avoid Fake Websites

Always use official websites for Aadhaar related services.

Use Masked Aadhaar

Use masked Aadhaar whenever you don't need to show full number.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is Aadhaar mandatory?

Aadhaar is required for many government and digital services, but usage depends on specific service requirements.


2. Can Aadhaar details be updated online?

Some demographic updates can be completed online while biometric updates require a visit to an Aadhaar centre.


3. Is sharing Aadhaar safe?

Share Aadhaar only with trusted organisations and never share OTP or authentication details.
